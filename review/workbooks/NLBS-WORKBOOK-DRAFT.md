## Exercise 2C: Active Listening Practice

*Playing catch — you ask (throw), they answer (throw back), you catch by actually listening.*

**The rule:** In your next conversation, before you respond to anything they say — pause for 3 full seconds. Let the silence do the work. Then reflect back what you heard before you say anything new.

**Reflect-back formula:** *"What I'm hearing is ___. Is that right?"*

# MODULE 3 — ASSUME THE SALE

*"The minute you ask 'what do you think?' — your sale is dead." — JM*

## Exercise 3A: Replace "What Do You Think?" Forever

*Every time you catch yourself about to say "what do you think?" — use one of these instead.*

Write your two-option close for your specific product/service:

**Scheduling a next step:**
"I'm available _______________ or _______________. Which works best for you?"

**Moving forward:**
"Here's what we're going to do — [describe next step]. Are you starting with ___ or ___?"

**Confirming a decision:**
"Most people in your situation go with ___. Would you like to do the same, or is ___ a better fit?"

---

## Exercise 3B: The 3 Reasons They Don't Buy

*Every objection fits into one of three buckets. Learn to diagnose fast.*

| Objection | Category | Your response |
|---|---|---|
| "I don't think you're the right fit for us." | **PERSON** — let them go gracefully | "No problem at all. Let me see if I can point you to someone who'd be a better match." |
| "I don't really understand how this works." | **PRODUCT** — re-explain the benefit | |
| "It's just too expensive right now." | **PRICE** — use Feel / Felt / Found | |
| "I need to think about it." | Usually PRICE in disguise | |
| "Now isn't a good time." | Usually PRICE or PERSON | |

---

## Exercise 3C: Master Feel / Felt / Found

*The only objection-handling framework you need. Practice until it's automatic.*

**The formula:**
- **Feel:** "I understand how you feel..."
- **Felt:** "A lot of my clients have felt the same way..."
- **Found:** "Here's what they found..." *(What happens if they DON'T move forward — the cost of inaction)*

# MODULE 4 — THE SECRET WEAPON: POST-NO RECOVERY

*"Don't leave after a no. That's where the magic is." — JM*

## Exercise 4A: The Post-No Script

*Practice this until it becomes reflex. Most salespeople never ask — which is why most don't grow.*

**When they say no, you say:**

"I completely respect that. Can I ask — just so I can learn and get better — why didn't you move forward tonight?"

Then: **stop talking.** Let them answer. A wall will come down.

**Write your natural version of this question (in your own voice):**

_______________________________________________
_______________________________________________

**What you do after they answer:**
- If it's price: re-open with Feel/Felt/Found
- If it's timing: ask what would need to change
- If it's product: clarify the benefit you clearly missed
- If it's you: thank them, let them go, and take a hard look at yourself
